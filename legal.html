To run emulators or certain decryption tools, the system requires cryptographic keys. These files are proprietary data owned by the hardware manufacturer and act as the foundational digital locks for the console's operating system. Under the DMCA (17 U.S.C. Key-dumping payloads are tools used to extract cryptographic keys from a console's motherboard. While the Right to Repair movement strongly argues that extracting data from hardware you legally own is a lawful exercise, major rightsholders disagree, routinely issuing aggressive DMCA takedown notices against the repositories that host these tools. To ensure the absolute stability of this website, we do not host compiled versions of these payloads.
